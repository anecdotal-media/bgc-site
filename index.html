What We’d Elevate
The Opportunities.
Six things holding the site back today — each one specific, fixable, and drawn straight from what you already told us. Tap to expand.
01Structure & navigation+
The same information lives in five different places, the copy runs on, and the top menu has grown to eight items that wrap onto two crowded rows. We’d rebuild the information architecture so every tab leads somewhere obvious — the clean, concise feel you admired at Greater Tarrant, but unmistakably yours.
02Six clubs, six homes+
Right now every location shares one crowded space. We’d give each of your six clubs its own page — its own schedule, events, hours, and personality — that your RD team can keep current in seconds, without waiting on the club directors.
03The giving experience+
Today there are several “Donate” buttons in one eyeful, and the one that works kicks donors out to a bare PayPal page — no suggested amounts, no monthly option, no impact framing. We’d replace it with one clean, on-brand giving flow (with a persistent donate button that follows the visitor) that keeps donors on your site and makes recurring gifts easy.
04Built for phones, found on Google+
The club schedules are saved as image files — unreadable on a phone, and invisible to Google and screen readers. Several “clickable” emails don’t actually click. We’d rebuild these as real, responsive content so the site works on the device most families actually use, and so search engines can finally find you.
05From stats to stories+
Your strongest material — the stories — is missing. There are no testimonials and no alumni journeys (a former club kid who’s now in college and back on staff each summer is exactly the story a parent or donor needs to see). People give to people. We’d put real faces, voices, and outcomes front and center, with the numbers as proof.
06The missing pieces+
A few things visitors look for simply aren’t there: your leadership and board on the About page, an FAQ for the questions parents actually ask (transportation, what to expect, how to register), and a low-pressure “get to know us” step — like an annual report — that builds trust before the donate ask.
